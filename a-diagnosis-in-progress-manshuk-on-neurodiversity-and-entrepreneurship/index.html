A Diagnosis in Progress: Understanding Begins at Home

ADHD   By Elissia Moullas

For Manshuk Kerey, a certified HR Specialist with international expertise, understanding neurodiversity didn’t begin with her own diagnosis, but with her son’s. When her son was formally diagnosed with ADHD, the experience became a mirror.

He’s a copy of me behaviorally. When I was his age, I didn’t struggle academically, but I did struggle with behaviors that weren’t well understood back then.

Looking back, I see why so many neurodivergent people shine so brightly. When we care about something, we go deep and perform at a very high level. Interest drives excellence.

A Career Interrupted

After moving to Canada and earning a graduate diploma from McGill University, Manshuk joined a global financial firm. When remote work began during COVID, the pressure intensified. She was eventually let go for “lack of attention to detail”, a decision that caught her by surprise.

After months of consistent performance and never requesting time off, I believed my reliability was clear. However, in the absence of trust, even minor technical issues can be misinterpreted as a lack of engagement.

Redefining Success Through Entrepreneurship

In 2022, Manshuk launched Phrcert.com, a platform designed to make HR certification preparation accessible to real professionals, especially those balancing demanding jobs, family responsibilities, and neurodivergent thinking styles.

When I create content, I think of people like me and my son. We need resources that are structured but flexible, challenging but achievable, and always clear.

A Message to Employers

Employees shouldn’t need a doctor’s note to deserve flexibility. We should review every request with empathy and create cultures where people feel safe to ask for what they need. When leaders model empathy and transparency, the entire culture shifts. Inclusion starts at the top.
